About Me

I am a second year PhD student in Economics at the University of Maryland College Park. My main research interest is on Macroeconomics. In particular, I am interested in how individual interactions among otherwise atomless individuals may give rise to interesting aggregate dynamics which are far from what representative agent or firm models may suggest. As such I consider myself as a macroeconomist in a very broad sense.

My previous research has focused on the measurement and impact of economic (policy) uncertainty in Chile and (briefly) on the economics of education. If you want to know more about that aspect, please visit the research section.
