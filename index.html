The Effects of Inflation on Relative Price Variability with Different Price Regimes during the Chilean Hyperinflation


with Rodrigo Cerda and Rolf Luders
We empirically assess the effects of inflation on the variability of relative prices during the Chilean hyperinflation episode in the early 1970s, a period marked by several price controls. Crucially, we study this relationship separating it for products which price was set by the government and those determined by market conditions. To do so, we use unique monthly data on the prices of 23 food products between November 1970 and December 1976. We find a positive impact of inflation on relative price variability. Importantly, this positive relationship is driven by products with a fixed price and not by those determined by market conditions.
